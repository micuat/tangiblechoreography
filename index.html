About

I am Naoto Hieda, engineer and emerging media artist. The project I am working on, Tangible Choreography, incorporates contemporary dance ideas into media and tangible interfaces.

Contemporary dance ideas go far beyond mere body movement. They might, for example, involve choreographing human cells or building a relationship between choreographer, dancer, audience, and space.

My mission is to materialize choreographic ideas as tangible interfaces, which audiences can touch and feel, by means of state-of-the-art technologies including brain-computer-interfaces, virtual reality, and haptics.

"Cellular Body (No Time To Fly)" by Naoto Hieda, 2015

Timeline

  • Phase 1: The 4th Choreographic Coding Lab (New York City, 2015)
  • Phase 2: danceWeb (Vienna, 2016)
  • Phase 3: PhD program at McGill University (Montréal, 2017-)

Over the next 5 years, I will be developing Tangible Choreography in an academic setting, through collaborations with choreographers, dancers, artists, and engineers. After my successful presentation, entitled "Cellular Body", at the 2015 Choreographic Coding Lab, I was selected among 1013 applicants to be one of the 66 participants in danceWeb, a workshop/residency program in Vienna. My participation in danceWeb will let me learn from choreographers at the forefront of contemporary dance, including Tino Sehgal, and build relationships with other emerging choreographers and dancers.

  • A: danceWeb and The Culture Programme of the European Commission cover two-thirds of the fee. Therefore, participants have to find fundings to cover the rest and other expenses. I did a lot of research, but unfortunately, I am not eligible for any of the fundings in Japan or Canada.
  • Q: Can I donate by wire transfer?

  • Q: Is the donation applicable to a tax credit?
  • A: Unfortunately, it will not considered to be a donation for tax credit in most of the countries. However, I can issue a receipt upon request, so please don't hesitate to email me.
  • Q: Why don't you use Kickstarter or other crowdfunding services?
  • A: Most of the crowdfunding services charge a fee (for example, Kickstarter takes 5 percent). I chose Paypal, which is slightly cheaper, so I can maximize your contribution.
