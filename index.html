About

I'm Naoto Hieda, an engineer and an emerging media artist. I propose Tangible Choreography, which incorporates contemporary dance ideas into media and tangible interfaces. Contemporary dance ideas are not limited to body movements, but for example, choreographing human cells or building a relationship among the choreographer, dancer, audience and space. My mission is to materialize choreographic ideas as tangible interfaces, which audiences can touch and feel, by means of state-of-the-art technologies including brain-computer-interfaces, virtual reality and haptics.

Timeline

  • Phase 1: The 4th Choreographic Coding Lab (New York City, 2015)
  • Phase 2: danceWeb (Vienna, 2016)
  • Phase 3: PhD program at McGill University (Montréal, 2017-)

A long-term timeline is to research Tangible Choreography in the academic domain through collaborations with choreographers, dancers, artists and engineers. Subsequent to the successful presentation "Cellular Body" at the Choreographic Coding Lab, Naoto is accepted to danceWeb, a workshop/residency program in Vienna to be one of the 66 participants out of 1013 applicants. Participation in danceWeb will help learning from choreographers in the forefront of the contemporary dance including Tino Sehgal and building relationship with other emerging choreographers and dancers.

  • Q: Why don't you use Kickstarter or other crowdfunding services?
